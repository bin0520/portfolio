Background

Duet is a Hong Kong–based social app that helps people "Meet People You Resonate With" — connecting users through shared interests and authentic connections. The product had traction in Asia and was looking to expand into the US market.

The challenge: Duet's team was unfamiliar with the US influencer landscape — creator pricing, audience preferences, and which content formats actually drive engagement. They also had limited budget to test and learn, so every dollar needed to count.

That's where I came in: to research the market, design a budget-aligned influencer strategy, and help them execute a campaign that could validate their US growth approach without over-spending.

Project Overview

As a freelance marketing consultant for Duet, I advised on a strategic pivot for their influencer campaign — moving from high-end branding to high-engagement community marketing. I delivered end-to-end strategy and execution support: refining the influencer selection process, advising on budget allocation, and helping maximize CAC efficiency.

Through data-driven analysis and strategic recommendations, I guided the shift from aspirational professionals to relatable micro-influencers, delivering a validated growth model that achieved significantly improved engagement rates and more authentic brand connections.

Goal

Find the right influencer mix for Duet's US launch — better engagement, stay within budget.

01

Discover

The Original Plan

Duet's initial influencer strategy focused on "High-End Professionals" — white-collar and elite creators who produced polished, aspirational content. The brand bet that premium production quality would resonate with the target audience.

It Didn't Work

Despite the polished look, audience engagement (likes/comments) stayed low. The content felt "distant" and lacked relatability — viewers scrolled past without connecting. Budget was spent, but results fell short.

The high-end influencer content was visually appealing but failed to connect emotionally. Low interaction rates led to inefficient budget use and a growing gap between spend and actual conversion intent.

02

Define

How I Developed the New Plan

After the original approach underperformed, I conducted market research to find a viable alternative. I explored the KOC (Key Opinion Consumer) and micro-influencer landscape: their pricing tiers, audience overlap with Duet's target demographic, and content formats that actually drive engagement. I then mapped this against Duet's budget constraints to design a realistic, executable plan.

Based on this research, I recommended a shift to "Authenticity over Aspiration": focus on University Students & Mid-tier Creators (KOCs) whose rates fit the budget and whose relatable content could drive higher engagement than polished professionals.

I advised prioritizing micro-influencers and student creators — daily routines, dorm life, study sessions — over aspirational settings. The new plan was budget-aligned, data-informed, and built to deliver both better engagement and cost efficiency.

03

Develop

Campaign Execution

I supported Duet in redesigning influencer selection, recruiting the new creator cohort, and running the campaign end-to-end — from strategy and briefing to performance tracking.

Strategy Shift

Shifted selection criteria from "Professional Status" to "Relatability & Storytelling", with new evaluation frameworks to match the campaign goals.

Creator Recruitment & Launch

Recruited and onboarded mid-tier student creators to produce daily routine content (dormitory life, study sessions, relatable moments) aligned with the campaign brief.

Key Campaign Elements

  • Selection Process: Moved from professional status assessment to a framework focused on relatability and storytelling ability
  • Budget & Rollout: Phased budget allocation to validate performance before scaling spend
  • Creator Profile: Focused on university students and mid-tier creators (micro-influencers/KOC) to balance engagement and cost efficiency
  • Content Themes: Content guidelines emphasizing daily routines, dormitory life, study sessions, and relatable moments
